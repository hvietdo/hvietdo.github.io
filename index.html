About me

My full name is Hoang Viet Do (and Đỗ Hoàng Việt in Vietnamese). I am currently a Master's student at Hanoi Institute of Mathematics (IMH) where I am fortunate to be supervised by Professor Ngo Viet Trung. My interests during Master's period are topics related to Algebraic Combinatorics. In particular, I am conducting a research into combinatorial properties of monomial ideals of polynomial rings. You can find my curriculum vitae at here.

Prior to Master's period, I studied at Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, VNUHCM-University of Science where I was supervised by Associate Professor Mai Hoang Bien. At university, my interests included the Commutative Algebra, Representation Theory of Finite Groups and Graph Theory. You can find my senior dissertation here. Its topic was intersection graphs of groups, where the group and graph theories intertwine.

Experience

  • Since July 2022, I have studied a topic related to monomial ideals under the guidance of Professor Ngo Viet Trung. Our work is a part of the training program for young mathematicians at IMH. After a half of the researching process, I completed a report about the connection between the normality of monomial ideals and integer rounding properties.
  • In Spring 2022, I was a teaching assistant at my university. I taught two courses: Linear Algebra (MTH00083) for three Computer Science classes, and Abstract Algebra (MTH00084) for one Mathematical class.
  • In Spring 2021, I had co-run a seminar on the theory of characters of finite groups. You can find more information about this seminar in my blog.
